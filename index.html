Landslide Susceptibility Mapping Project - [Group 9]

Welcome to our project on Landslide Susceptibility Mapping, a collaborative effort by our group,
a team of geoinformatics students at Politecnico di Milano University.
Our goal is to develop a comprehensive framework for predicting landslide susceptibility in our assigned case study.
As geoinformatics students, we are passionate about applying spatial analysis and Geographic Information Systems (GIS)
to solve real-world problems.
For our project, we are combining cutting-edge technologies and techniques with traditional geological knowledge to better understand the factors that contribute to landslide risk.

Objectives:
Our Project aims to:
Identify the key factors that influence landslide susceptibility.
Develop a predictive model for landslide risk assessment using GIS and machine learning algorithms.
We hope that our project will make a positive impact on the field of geoinformatics
And contribute to reducing the risk of landslides in the assigned case study.

Introduction

Case Study
The study area is centered around Aprica, Lombardy, Italy, known for its challenging terrain and susceptibility to landslides.
This picturesque area is characterized by its stunning alpine scenery, including dense forests, rugged mountains, and a serene lake.
The case study outlines a region known for its rich biodiversity and diverse ecosystems,
Making it an ideal location for environmental and geographical studies.
Methodology
We are employing a multi-criteria decision analysis approach, integrating multiple datasets and spatial analysis techniques,
to assess the complex relationships between geological, geomorphological, and environmental factors. Our methodology involves:
* Data collection: gathering relevant data on landslides, topography, geology, hydrology, climate, and land use
* Data processing: cleaning, preprocessing, and integrating the datasets
* Spatial analysis: applying GIS techniques to analyze the relationships between variables
* Model development: training machine learning models to predict landslide susceptibility
Contribution
Our project contributes to the advancement of landslide risk assessment and management in several ways:
* Developing a new framework for landslide susceptibility mapping that can be applied to other areas
* Providing insights into the complex relationships between geological, geomorphological, and environmental factors
* Offering a decision-making tool for stakeholders and policymakers

WebGIS

The WebGIS section showcases our interactive map application, which allows users to explore landslide susceptibility in the study area.
Users can interact with various layers of data, including slope, soil type, and historical landslide occurrences, to better understand the factors influencing landslide risks.
Our WebGIS platform is designed to be user-friendly and informative, providing valuable insights for researchers, planners, and the general public.
